PROJECT: SOULAMORE

Why We Exist

Not a business plan. A response to quiet moments.

01 // THE ORIGIN

We make space for the messy stuff.

Soulamore came from the realization that everything looks fine from the outside, but something often feels heavy inside.

We live in a world where conversations are fast, curated, and filtered. Yet, the emotions we struggle with — loneliness, self-doubt, pressure — are slow and messy. Soulamore exists to make space for those conversations again.

Aditya Harsh Architect & Founder

02 // THE ARCHITECT'S PERSPECTIVE

Designing Emotional Spaces

Soulamore is founded by Aditya Harsh, an architect by training.


"Architecture taught me how to design spaces that people move through every day — offices, campuses, public places. But somewhere along that journey, I realized something uncomfortable: we design buildings carefully, but often ignore the emotional load people carry inside them."

"Soulamore is my attempt to design a different kind of space. Not built with concrete or glass, but with empathy, honesty, and shared experiences."
03 // STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY

What We Are Not

  • A mental health clinic.
  • A replacement for therapy.
  • A place for quick fixes.
  • Just "motivational quotes."

What We Are

  • A safe space to share what you carry.
  • Peer conversations, not performances.
  • Emotional honesty, not perfection.
  • Community before content.
