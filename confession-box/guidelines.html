Guidelines, Safety & Moderation

The Confession Box is built on trust. These guidelines exist to protect everyone involved: the person sharing, the people reading, and the community as a whole.

Why this page exists

People share thoughts here that they may not say out loud anywhere else. That makes this space meaningful — and also sensitive.

We want you to feel safe enough to share, while keeping the platform responsible and grounded.

What it IS

  • A space to share thoughts you carry quietly
  • Expression without fear of judgement
  • Feeling less alone in isolation
  • Conversations rooted in empathy

You don’t need perfect words. Just honesty.

What it is NOT

  • A substitute for professional therapy
  • A crisis intervention service
  • A place for diagnosis or medical advice
  • A platform for harassment or hate

Guidelines for Sharing

✔ Do This

Share from experience: Speak in your own voice about what you feel or struggle with.

Be Respectful: Toward yourself, others reading, and people mentioned indirectly.

❌ Do Not Include

  • Names, phone numbers, addresses
  • Hate speech or abusive language
  • Graphic or explicit sexual content
  • Promotion of self-harm or violence
  • Medical claims or instructions

Anonymity & Moderation

Privacy:

Confessions can be submitted anonymously. We do not require real names. Personal identifiers are removed if detected.

How we Moderate:

Every confession goes through human moderation before being shared. We check for emotional safety and community wellbeing.

Moderation is NOT: Judgement or censorship of "bad" feelings.
Moderation IS: A safety check to protect vulnerable readers.

Community Responses

If you are commenting on a confession:

  • Lead with empathy, not advice
  • Avoid minimising someone’s experience
  • Do not diagnose or label
  • Respect boundaries

Sensitive & High-Risk Content

If a confession indicates active suicidal intent, immediate danger, or severe distress, we may choose not to publish it publicly.

Soulamore is not equipped to handle emergencies — and being honest about that is part of keeping people safe. If you are in danger, please contact local emergency services.

Reporting Concerns

If you see content that feels harmful, abusive, or unsafe, please report it immediately.
